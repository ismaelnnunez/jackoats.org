Research

Like most things, determining whether or not housing is unaffordable for many people is not very straightforward. Determining whether it's a crisis or not is probably even less so, but it's also probably not important. What is important is determining if it's a problem.

Defining housing affordability

What does it mean to be able to “afford housing”? What does it mean to be able to afford anything, for that matter? Well…

  • To afford something means literally “to be able to pay for something” (https://www.oed.com/search/dictionary/?scope=Entries&q=afford), but that definition isn't all that helpful.
  • Perhaps what would be more helpful is looking at the cost of housing divided by income. This can be applied to both renters and owners. It might be the case that all sorts of expenses and their ratio to income are astronomically high but that might indicate that there are other affordability problems too, or maybe that wages are too low. But for the purposes of this research, we only want to look at housing.
